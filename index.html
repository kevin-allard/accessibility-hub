Accessibility Resources Hub

Supporting customer-facing teams with accurate, consistent, and low-risk responses to accessibility inquiries from districts, schools, and partners.

Important: Always use approved language and documentation. Do not interpret or summarize accessibility compliance.

Start Here

Use these guardrails whenever responding to accessibility-related customer inquiries.

✅ Always Do

  • Share the latest ACR for the relevant product.
  • Use approved response language.
  • Refer customers to documentation rather than summarizing it.
  • Offer follow-up on specific questions.

❌ Do NOT

  • State that a product is “fully compliant” unless explicitly documented.
  • Interpret ACR findings as “mostly compliant” or similar shorthand.
  • Commit to timelines such as April 2026.
  • Make guarantees or compare products casually.
Rule of thumb: If it is not written in the ACR or approved guidance, do not say it.
1

Step 1: Identify the scope

  • Confirm the exact product, program, edition, and format involved.
  • Determine whether the request is about a platform, digital books, print materials, or all three.
  • Use the Accessibility by Program tool when a district is asking about a named program.
2

Step 2: Gather the documentation

  • Locate the latest ACRs for the relevant platforms.
  • Check whether accessible digital books / PDFs are available through the Clear Learning Center.
  • Check whether NIMAS is available for the related print titles.
3

Step 3: Respond using approved language

  • Use the approved response template as your default starting point.
  • Attach or link the relevant documentation.
  • Let the documents speak for themselves.
4

Step 4: Escalate if needed

  • Escalate questions involving commitments, guarantees, legal language, or interpretation of results.
  • Escalate if multiple products or delivery paths create uncertainty.
  • When in doubt, ask before responding externally.

Internal Context (For Staff Only)
Some Carnegie Learning platforms, including MATHia, have evolved over a long period of time, with origins predating current accessibility standards such as WCAG 2.1. As a result, accessibility improvements are being implemented incrementally as part of ongoing platform updates and modernization efforts.

This context helps explain why ACRs may reflect a mix of support levels across criteria. This information is for internal understanding only and should not be shared directly with customers. Always rely on ACR documentation and approved messaging when responding externally.

NIMAS supports accessibility for print materials and alternate format delivery. Accessible digital books and PDFs delivered through the Clear Learning Center are separate from NIMAS. Neither should be described as equivalent to WCAG conformance for Carnegie Learning web platforms.

Accessibility Philosophy & Policy

Carnegie Learning is committed to providing equitable access to our products and experiences for all learners. Accessibility is a core design principle and an ongoing responsibility across product development, content creation, and customer support.

Our Principles

  • Evolving Portfolio: Our products have been developed and modernized over time. As accessibility standards and technologies evolve, our portfolio reflects a range of support levels while we continue to remediate and enhance accessibility across systems.
  • Design for Access: We aim to design products that are usable by the widest range of learners, including those using assistive technologies.
  • Standards Alignment: We align to WCAG 2.1/2.2 and applicable regulations (e.g., Section 508) and document support via ACRs.
  • Transparency: We communicate accessibility through ACRs and approved guidance rather than informal summaries.
  • Consistency: We provide clear, repeatable guidance for customer-facing teams to ensure accurate messaging.

Our Policy

  • Document-First Communication: ACRs are the authoritative source for accessibility support and must be shared in responses.
  • No Overstatement: Staff must not claim compliance or timelines unless explicitly documented.
  • Third-Party Clarity: Accessibility for third-party tools (e.g., Learnosity, KITABOO) is represented by vendor ACRs.
  • Program-Level Clarity: Responses should reflect the full delivery model (platforms, tools, digital books, and print/NIMAS).
  • Escalation: Legal language, commitments, or interpretation requests must be routed to Accessibility leads.

Continuous Improvement

  • Regular third-party audits and internal testing with assistive technologies.
  • Backlog-driven remediation and iterative releases.
  • Retesting and validation following updates.

What This Means for Customers

  • Districts review ACRs to determine fit within their accessibility plans.
  • Alternative pathways (e.g., NIMAS, accessible digital books) may support access where needed.
  • Accessibility is an ongoing effort; documentation reflects current, point-in-time support.
Accessibility is an ongoing commitment. We continuously improve our products and documentation, and we partner with customers to support equitable access.

What to Say / What Not to Say

Use these examples to respond consistently and reduce risk.

Are Carnegie Learning products WCAG 2.1 AA compliant?

Say:
“The ACR provides a detailed, criterion-level view of accessibility support. I recommend reviewing that document for the most accurate information.”
Do NOT say:
“Yes, we are compliant.”
“Mostly compliant.”
“We meet WCAG.”

Will Carnegie Learning be compliant by April 2026?

Say:
“We are actively improving accessibility across our products. Please refer to the ACR and roadmap for the most current information.”
Do NOT say:
“Yes, we will be compliant by April 2026.”
“We are on track to meet that deadline.”

Can districts still use Carnegie Learning products if they are not fully conformant?

Say:
“Districts typically review ACRs and determine how products fit within their accessibility plans, including accommodations or alternative formats where needed.”
Do NOT say:
“There is no risk.”
“It’s compliant anyway.”

What about print materials and digital books?

Say:
“For applicable print programs, we provide NIMAS files that enable accessible formats such as braille, large print, and audio. Some programs also include accessible digital books delivered through the Clear Learning Center.”
Do NOT say:
“Print materials are WCAG compliant.”
“NIMAS makes us compliant.”
“All digital books are accessible across all programs.”

Can I summarize the ACR for the customer?

Say:
“I recommend sharing the ACR directly, as it provides the most complete and accurate information.”
Do NOT say:
“It mostly supports accessibility.”
“There are only minor issues.”

Why are districts asking more about accessibility now?

Say:
“Many districts are strengthening procurement and compliance review processes related to digital accessibility, which is increasing requests for ACRs and related documentation.”
Do NOT say:
“Everyone has to be fully compliant by one fixed date.”
“This only affects some states.”

When to Escalate

Contact the Accessibility Team in any of the following scenarios:

Escalate if the customer asks for:

  • A formal compliance statement
  • Contract or legal language
  • Interpretation of ACR results
  • Product-specific guarantees or commitments

Also escalate if:

  • You are unsure how to respond
  • The request includes procurement or district audit language
  • The customer asks for a timeline commitment
  • The inquiry spans multiple products or formats
